Addressing The Obesity Epidemic

The obesity epidemic has systematically begun to erode the financial stability of the American health-care system. More than two-thirds of adults are overweight, and at least one-third are considered obese (5% are morbidly obese). Obesity is linked to the increasing incidence of heart disease, diabetes, Alzheimer’s, cancer and other afflictions [REFERENCES HERE.]

The saying “you are what you eat” does not quite capture the full extent of the impact that poor nutrition can have on a family and their lineage. Food is not only used by the body for nourishment but it also influences the expression of genes that are passed down hereditary lines. Research in epigenetics shows that what we eat governs the health of our children and grandchildren. Rather than just calories, food is interpreted by our body as a form of “information” that conveys vital clues regarding our environment.
